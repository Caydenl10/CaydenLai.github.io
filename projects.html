Challenges & Solutions

The most significant challenge I encountered was [CHALLENGE, e.g., sensor crosstalk — when multiple ultrasonic sensors fired simultaneously, their pulses interfered with each other, causing erroneous distance readings]. Initial attempts using [APPROACH 1, e.g., a simple polling loop that triggered all sensors in rapid succession] resulted in [PROBLEM, e.g., phantom obstacle detections that caused the robot to stop in open space]. After researching [RESOURCE, e.g., the HC-SR04 datasheet and community forums], I discovered that the sensors required a minimum recovery time of [TIME, e.g., 60ms] between firings when placed in close proximity.

I resolved this by [SOLUTION, e.g., implementing a staggered interrupt-driven firing sequence with a 75ms guard interval between each sensor, triggered by a hardware timer rather than a software delay]. This not only eliminated the crosstalk but also [ADDITIONAL BENEFIT, e.g., freed the main loop from blocking, making the control response more responsive overall]. A secondary challenge was [CHALLENGE 2, e.g., battery voltage sag under motor load causing the microcontroller to reset mid-operation]. I addressed this by [SOLUTION 2, e.g., adding bulk capacitors across the power rail and using a dedicated 5V regulator for the logic circuitry]. These two fixes transformed the system from unreliable to consistently functional — a lesson in the importance of thorough hardware debugging.
